Dhurandhar facts vs fiction: Here’s how much Aditya Dhar and Ranveer Singh’s film is inspired by real events

Dhurandhar 1 and 2 draw inspiration from several real-life geopolitical events and conflicts in India and Pakistan. Know facts vs fiction in the film.

Dhurandhar has officially arrived in theatres, and fans are eager to watch Hamza’s quest for revenge. The movie draws inspiration from several real-life geopolitical events and conflicts in India and Pakistan, including Operation Lyari, the 26/11 terror attack, the 2016 banknote demonetisation and much more. Know what fact is and what fiction is in Dhurandhar 2.

REAL EVENTS

Major Iqbal

Major Iqbal, played by Arjun Rampal, is reportedly based on Ilyas Kashmiri, who was a Pakistani Special Forces Operator turned Islamist jihadist militant leader. He was reportedly associated with the 26/11 Mumbai attack and the 2010 Pune bombing, similar to Major Iqbal in Dhurandhar.

SP Chaudhry Aslam

Played by Sanjay Dutt, SP Chaudhry Aslam was based on a real-life figure as well. Like in Dhurandhar 2, he was killed in a suicide car bombing. However, in the movie, it is shown that Aslam died due to Hamza’s conspiracy.

Ajay Sanyal

Ajay Sanyal, played by R Madhavan, is based on Ajit Doval, who has been the National Security Advisor of India (NSA) since 2014. Under his tenure, Operation Hot Pursuit, the Balakot airstrike and Operation Sindoor happened. However, Operation Dhurandhar is purely a fiction.

Jameel Jamali

Jameel Jamali is based on Nabil Gabol, who was a member of the National Assembly from 2002 to 2015. There is a twist about his character, which is purely fiction as well.

Uzair Balcoch

Uzair Baloch, played by Danish Pador, is based on the real gangster of the same name. The fate of Uzair is different in Dhurandhar 2, which is also not true.

Bade Sahab

Bade Sahab turned out to be Dawood Ibrahim, who is also a real-life gangster. It is reported that Dawood Ibrahim currently lives in Karachi, but Pakistan’s government has denied it. It is true that he is said to have a huge influence on several terrorist activities.

Death of terreorists

All of these characters are based on real-life people. The deaths of these characters are similar to what happened in real life. However, the association of Hamza with their deaths is entirely fictional.

Indian Banknote Demonetisation

In 2016, the Indian government announced the demonetisation of all Rs 500 and Rs 1000 notes and also announced the issuance of new Rs 500 and Rs 2000 notes. This was done in order to reduce the use of illicit and counterfeit cash to fund illegal activity and terrorism, and increase cashless transactions. This is linked to Hamza’s mission in Dhurandhar 2 when he kills Khanani, who owed Dawood over 60,000 crores. This scene is also a fiction.

REHEMAN DAKAIT

Rehman Dakait, born Abdul Rehman in 1976, grew up in Lyari, one of Karachi's oldest and poorest neighbourhoods - an area long synonymous with crime, gang wars and a deep-rooted crime-police nexus.
